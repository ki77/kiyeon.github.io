Exercise

Even as a busy student or office worker, regular exercise is critical. Our bodies are machines that will break down if not prepared.

The health department recommends getting around 30-60 minutes of exercise per day, which can be divided in chunks and spread out over the week. While this is a good goal, it's better to get at least a bit of exercise than no exercise. It's helpful to commit to exercising at a consistent time (like every morning at 7:00-7:30AM, before things get busy).

I like to do an evenly-spaced combination of strength (weight-lifting), cardio (running), flexibility (yoga), and fun sports (basketball, tennis). Here's my weekly exercise schedule:

Mon Tues Wed Thurs Fri Sat Sun
weights run weights run weights sports run, yoga

StrongLifts.com has a great in-depth guide to weight-lifting. Read the About page story to get motivated and start the StrongLifts 5x5 workout.

The NYT has a nice guide on how to start running. The key point is to run around 30 minutes three days a week.
